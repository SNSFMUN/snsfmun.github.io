UN International Children's Emergency Fund

Agenda - Ensuring Access to Primary Education for All.

Primary education translates into a robust foundation for children to help develop skills required for a better living. The unequitable access to education is more defined in developing nations which is a vicious circle of poor living standards and poverty. The issue lies not only as access to primary education but also the quality of education imparted herewith. As per the data available a whopping 53% of children (aged 10 and below) in low and middle income are unable to comprehend a simple story by the end of their primary years. The cultural aspects, gender inequality and social norms are a few hindrances in the way of accessing primary education for everybody. Though the gap in the actual percentage globally in the past 70 years has been heartening (reduced from 50 % of children not having primary education to 11 % as of today's date), the universalization of primary education, especially in developing countries, is far from the desired level. In most cases the basic literacy, numeracy and transferable skills are not adequate to grow and thrive in the new world order where these skills are pertinent to make a living. The access to education is the foremost and perquisite to alleviate a substantial population from the grips of poverty and bringing acceptable levels of social cohesion. The problem is pronounced in the most marginalized sections of the population and children displaced by war and it requires a political commitment by the global leaders to ensure the access available to basic right of education which includes quality aspects, parental engagement and willingness. The committee will discuss the strategies and relevance of primary education for all.
