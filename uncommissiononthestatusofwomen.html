UN Commission on the Status of Women

Agenda - Women's Economic Empowerment in a Changing World of Work.

The Commission on the Status of Women (CSW) was formed in 1947 comprising 15 all women government representatives for the promotion of equal status of women and empowering them by working closely with NGOs, under the UN Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC). In 1987, following the Third World Conference on Women in Nairobi, the Commission took to promoting the economic and social issues for women’s empowerment. To foster global awareness of women’s issues and canvass for equal rights for women including making amendments to discriminatory legislation related to women’s access to education, equal job opportunities, equal compensation at work place, and promoting use of more inclusive or gender neutral language and supportive work environment.This is essential now more than ever as the percentage of women in active workforce has begun to show a decline.To address the emerging needs of the women playing diverse social and economic roles, it’s only a natural progression that special attention is paid to this unprecedented scenario.
